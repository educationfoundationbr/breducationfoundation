Grants

Fueling creative, innovative learning.

The Education Foundation of Bridgewater-Raritan provides grants for unique scientific, cultural, artistic, and scholastic programs not available through public funding. Your project idea can become a real experience for students across the district.

How to Apply

Three steps to submit a Mini Grant.

Applications are reviewed by Foundation trustees and district leaders. Focus on student impact and alignment with core principles.

  1. Define the project

    Share your title, summary, scope, and which schools or student groups will benefit.

  2. Build the budget

    List items, vendors, unit costs, quantities, and totals to show how funds will be used.

  3. Confirm approvals

    Identify your core funding area, gather principal approval, and submit the application.

Mini Grant Application Checklist

"*" indicates required fields.

  • Title and summary of the Mini Grant purpose.
  • Scope, schools impacted, and number of students served.
  • Lead contact details and co-applicants.
  • Budget with itemized descriptions and totals.
  • Core principles alignment and project timeline.

Requirements

  • Applicants must be BRRSD faculty or staff.
  • Mini Grants: up to $750 for classroom projects.
  • Collaborative projects: up to $1,500.
  • Projects must align with at least one core funding area.
  • Principal approval is required before submission.

Review Process

  1. Applications are reviewed by Foundation trustees and district leaders.
  2. Projects are evaluated on student impact, innovation, and feasibility.
  3. Applicants are notified once funding decisions are finalized.
  4. Recipients provide a brief impact summary, photos, and a short video.
